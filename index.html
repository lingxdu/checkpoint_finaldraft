Flexible Oasis

Vanilla Town Planning and Design in Shuangwan

  • Location: Gansu, China
  • Period: October - December, 2019
  • Designer: Lingxiao Du
  • Type: Individual work

Introduction

Shuangwan is surrounded by desert on three sides, that is why it is called "Oasis in the Desert". There are various types of agricultural industries, such as vanilla cultivation, crop cultivation, vegetable and fruit planting, and sheep breeding. Meanwhile, human relics, the Sanjiao City and the Guandi Temple, are scattered throughout the town. These agricultural industries have contributed to the economic development of the town, and together with the cultural monuments, they have become an attraction for the development of tourism in Shuangwan. Therefore, this planning and design is based on these two advantages. At the same time, this plan also focuses on solving the current situation of water shortage in Shuangwan.

Gansu

Gansu is a province in Northwest China. Its capital and largest city is Lanzhou, in the southeast part of the province. The seventh-largest administrative district by area at 453,700 square kilometres (175,200 sq mi), Gansu lies between the Tibetan and Loess plateaus and borders Mongolia (Govi-Altai Province), Inner Mongolia and Ningxia to the north, Xinjiang and Qinghai to the west, Sichuan to the south and Shaanxi to the east. The Yellow River passes through the southern part of the province. Part of Gansu's territory is located in the Gobi Desert. The Qilian mountains are located in the south of the Province.
