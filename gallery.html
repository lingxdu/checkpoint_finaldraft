Site Analysis

Collage

The site faces four main problems. First, it fails to maximize the use of water resources. Since precipitation is small but evaporation is large in Shuangwan, it is necessary to make full use of water resources. Second, many houses are in disrepair, because those houses are mostly made of bricks, and they have gone through a century. At the same time, some houses are abandoned for a long time due to the outflow of young people in the town. Third, there is a shortage of parking spaces, hotels, and restaurants. Fourth, the roads on the site are mostly old cement pavement, with rubbish piled up on both sides from time to time.

Function analysis

The site will be designed with a spatial structure of one main axis, one commercial axis, and two landscape axes.

Water recycling system

To solve the problem of water shortage, the design considers collecting gray water and rainwater, filtering them and reusing them.

Mobility system

The site is divided into three levels of roadways considering motor vehicle, bicycle and pedestrian peers.

Open space design analysis

The open spaces in Shuangwan are divided into four categories: courtyard, park, plaza, and sports ground. These areas can meet the needs of residents for recreation or sports at different times of the day, such as singing, reading newspapers, playing chess, talking, picnic, playing basketball and baseball, running, etc. The semi-open space includes two street spaces that provide residents and visitors with space for coffee and outdoor dining. The green buffer and recreation spaces are designed to cultivate native plants, such as pagoda tree, pear, and marigold that also serve as rainwater storage. These open and semi-open spaces are located around the main pedestrian street and business street, providing easy access to residents and visitors and greatly enhancing accessibility.
